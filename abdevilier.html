AB de Villiers, also known as "Mr. 360°", is one of the most versatile and beloved cricketers in the world.

Known for his innovative stroke play and ability to hit the ball to all corners of the ground, he redefined modern batting.

He made his international debut in 2004 and quickly became a key pillar in the South African lineup.

De Villiers is the fastest player to score 50, 100, and 150 in ODIs, breaking multiple records with his fearless approach.

In 2015, he scored the fastest ODI century in just 31 balls against the West Indies.

Despite his aggressive batting style, he maintained a high average and strike rate across all formats.

He was equally skilled in Tests, adapting his game to play long innings under pressure when needed.

AB was also an exceptional fielder and wicketkeeper when the team needed him to step in behind the stumps.

He played a vital role in South Africa’s successes in the mid-2000s to 2010s, including memorable World Cup and Test series performances.

Fans admired not only his cricketing skills but also his humility, sportsmanship, and team-first attitude.

Even after retiring from international cricket in 2018, he remained a force in franchise leagues like the IPL.

He played several match-winning knocks for Royal Challengers Bangalore and earned massive fan followings worldwide.

AB’s ability to innovate on the fly set him apart — reverse sweeps, scoops, ramps, and more were all part of his arsenal.

He remains one of the few players to average over 50 in both ODIs and Tests while maintaining a high strike rate.

His camaraderie with teammates and clean reputation off the field earned him respect from opponents as well.

AB continues to mentor young cricketers and contribute to the game through commentary and coaching.

His legacy as Mr. 360° will be remembered as one of the most entertaining and graceful eras in cricket.

For many, he wasn’t just a player — he was an artist with the bat.
