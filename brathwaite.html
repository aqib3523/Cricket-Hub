Match Summary

In one of the most iconic T20 finals ever, Carlos Brathwaite carved his name into cricketing folklore.

It was April 3, 2016, in Kolkata. West Indies were chasing 156 to win against a spirited England side.

The situation looked grim — they needed 19 runs off the last over, with Ben Stokes bowling.

Enter Carlos Brathwaite — tall, powerful, and calm under pressure.

What followed was one of the most explosive finishes in cricket history.

First ball – SIX over deep backward square leg. 13 needed.

Second ball – SIX straight down the ground. 7 to go.

Third ball – SIX over long off. Game tied!

Fourth ball – SIX again! West Indies were champions. The crowd erupted.

Brathwaite finished on 34* off just 10 balls, with 4 sixes in the final over.

He didn’t just win a game — he delivered a cricketing moment for the ages.

Marlon Samuels, who anchored the innings with a gritty 85*, was named Player of the Match.

But Brathwaite was the hero everyone remembered.

Ian Bishop’s famous commentary — “Carlos Brathwaite! Remember the name!” — became immortal.

It was West Indies’ second men’s T20 World Cup title after their 2012 triumph.

The win also capped a sensational day where the West Indies U-19s and Women’s teams also had recent world victories.

The celebrations were wild and emotional, with players dancing, hugging, and soaking in the moment.

Brathwaite's 4 sixes weren’t just power — they were poetry under pressure.

The performance remains one of the most clutch finishes in cricket history.
