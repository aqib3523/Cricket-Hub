Shoaib Akhtar, famously known as the "Rawalpindi Express," is one of the most feared fast bowlers in cricket history.

He holds the record for the fastest delivery ever bowled in cricket — a mind-blowing 161.3 km/h against England in 2003.

Born in Rawalpindi, Pakistan, his raw pace, aggression, and intimidating run-up made him a global sensation.

He made his international debut in 1997 and soon rattled top-class batters with his express deliveries.

Shoaib was especially dangerous with the new ball and reverse swing under lights in ODIs.

One of his most iconic spells was in 1999 when he clean bowled Rahul Dravid and Sachin Tendulkar in successive balls.

Though plagued by injuries, he often made remarkable comebacks and delivered match-winning performances.

He played key roles in Pakistan's triumphs, particularly in the early 2000s, in both Tests and ODIs.

In World Cups, he consistently bowled with pace and aggression, thrilling fans worldwide.

His fierce personality and passionate patriotism made him a favorite among Pakistani supporters.

Despite criticism for his off-field antics, he never shied away from expressing his love for the game.

Shoaib had rivalries with some of the best batters of his era including Sehwag, Tendulkar, Ponting, and Lara.

Off the field, he’s a charismatic personality and continues to entertain audiences through commentary and his YouTube channel.

He inspired a generation of young fast bowlers in Pakistan with his fiery style and unmatched pace.

Post-retirement, Shoaib Akhtar remains a strong voice in cricket media, often making bold and honest statements.

He was more than just speed — he had control, reverse swing mastery, and match-winning spells under pressure.

His unique action and energy made him one of the most watchable and thrilling cricketers of his time.

Whether you loved him or feared him, Shoaib Akhtar left an undeniable impact on world cricket.
