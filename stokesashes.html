Match Summary

Ben Stokes’ 135 not out at Headingley in 2019 is regarded as one of the greatest Test innings of all time.

England was chasing a mammoth 359 against Australia to stay alive in the Ashes series.

At one point, England was 286/9, still needing 73 runs to win with only one wicket remaining.

Stokes, already on a brilliant 60+, turned into a beast of composure, aggression, and belief.

He unleashed a flurry of boundaries and sixes, manipulating the strike with tailender Jack Leach.

With every ball, the tension grew — Australia missed run-out chances and dropped a key catch.

Stokes reverse-swept, drove, and powered England to an impossible win.

Jack Leach held firm with 1* off 17 balls, famously cleaning his glasses between overs.

Stokes hit the winning runs in dramatic fashion, sparking wild celebrations in the crowd and the dressing room.
