Match Summary

On September 19, 2007, Yuvraj Singh etched his name in cricketing history during the ICC T20 World Cup in South Africa.

India was playing England in a group-stage clash at Kingsmead, Durban.

What ignited the fire was a heated exchange between Yuvraj and Andrew Flintoff.

Fueled by adrenaline, Yuvraj took the next over – bowled by Stuart Broad – and did the unthinkable.

First ball: over midwicket – SIX!

Second ball: over square leg – SIX!

Third ball: straight down the ground – SIX!

Fourth ball: over point – SIX!

Fifth ball: over square again – SIX!

Sixth ball: full toss smashed over midwicket – SIX!

The crowd erupted. Yuvraj had just hit six sixes in an over – the first to do so in T20 internationals.

He reached his fifty in just 12 balls – still the fastest in any T20I.

India posted a massive total of 218/4 and went on to win the match comfortably.

It was a turning point in India’s campaign that eventually led to them winning the inaugural T20 World Cup.

Yuvraj’s innings was explosive, fearless, and a celebration of clean hitting.

The knock not only changed the game but defined Yuvraj’s legacy as one of India’s most destructive hitters.

His bat swing, swagger, and timing were unmatched that night.

The over is now immortalized as one of the greatest moments in cricket history.

Even years later, fans revisit it for chills, nostalgia, and inspiration.
